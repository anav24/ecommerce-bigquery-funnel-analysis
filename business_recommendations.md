# Business Recommendations

## 1. Improve product-page conversion

The largest funnel drop-off happened between product views and add-to-cart activity. Only 20.48% of users who viewed a product added one to their cart, meaning most interested shoppers left before taking the next purchase step.

Recommendation:
Review product pages with high views but low conversion. Improve product descriptions, images, pricing clarity, availability messaging, and call-to-action placement.

## 2. Investigate January performance decline

Revenue dropped from $160,555 in December to $57,350 in January. Purchase rate also fell from 1.82% to 1.02%.

Recommendation:
Review whether this decline was caused by seasonality, reduced promotions, lower traffic quality, product availability, or checkout issues after the holiday shopping period.

## 3. Review high-view products with low purchase conversion

Several products had high views but very low or zero purchase conversion, including Google F/C Long Sleeve Tee Charcoal, Google Women's Striped L/S, and Google Eco Tee Black.

Recommendation:
Check these products for stock issues, pricing problems, product-page quality, or mismatches between customer expectations and the final checkout experience.

## 4. Continue investing in top traffic channels

Google organic search and direct traffic generated the most revenue. Google organic produced $95,775 in revenue, while direct traffic produced $79,650.

Recommendation:
Continue optimizing organic search visibility and brand/direct traffic. These channels appear to drive the strongest overall revenue volume.

## 5. Optimize mobile while maintaining desktop performance

Desktop generated the most revenue at $208,815 because it had the most sessions. However, mobile had the highest purchase rate at 1.64%.

Recommendation:
Continue improving the mobile shopping experience, since mobile users converted slightly better. At the same time, maintain desktop performance because it still drives the largest revenue total.
